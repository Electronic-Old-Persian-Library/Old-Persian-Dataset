DPb

Achaemenid Royal Inscriptions: collection of Old Persian cuneiform texts from the sixth, fifth, and fourth centuries BCE, left by the Achaemenid kings on their official monuments.DPb, inscription from Persepolis

Inscription DPb

Inscription (in one line) on the garment of Darius on a relief in his Palace, and (in four lines) above the portrait of Xerxes on the reliefs of his Palace. (The picture shows the latter, fallen upside down.).

Dârayavauš \ xšâya
thiya \ vazraka \ Viš
tâspahyâ \ puça 
\ Haxâmanišiya \


Darius, the great king, son of Hystaspes, an Achaemenid.

Literature

Pierre Lecoq, Les inscriptions de la Perse achéménide (1997 Paris)
