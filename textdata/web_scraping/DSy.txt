Achaemenid Royal Inscriptions: collection of Old Persian cuneiform texts from the sixth, fifth, and fourth centuries BCE, left by the Achaemenid kings on their official monuments.DSv, inscription on a column base from Susa

DSy

Inscription in Old Persian, Elamite, and Babylonian, written on a column base, which is now in the National Archaeological Museum in Tehran. The text is identical to DSb (where you will find a drawing).

adam \ Dârayavauš XŠ \ vazraka XŠ XŠyânâm
XŠ DHyunâm XŠ \ ahyâyâ BUyâ \ Vi
štâspahyâ \ puça \ Haxâmanišiya


I am Darius, the great king, king of kings, king of all nations, king of this earth, son of Hystaspes, the Achaemenid.

Literature

Pierre Lecoq, Les inscriptions de la Perse achéménide (1997 Paris)
